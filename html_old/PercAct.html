Perception and

Action

 

One of the major research tracks in the Cognitive program focuses on the study of perception and action. Broadly, this group is concerned with questions of how information from different sensory modalities, including vision, haptics, and kinesthesis, acts to give rise to specific perceptions and to guide motor actions including reaching, obstacle avoidance, and eye movements. Specific issues concern the development of these processes from infancy, their interaction with circadian biological rhythms, the underlying neural substrate, the influence of attention, and the resolution of various information-processing costs. Students entering our program gain experience with a variety of methodologies including behavioral measures, brain-wave recording, eye-movement monitoring, and computational modeling.

The Perception and Action group collaborates broadly both within Penn State University and with members of other Universities throughout the US and the world (link to list of collaborators). Students, faculty, and visiting scholars participate in a number of regular speaker series in which research related to perception and action is presented and discussed. These include meetings of the Action Club, meetings of the Brainy Bunch (alternate Fridays; see the Cognitive Calendar for details), and the SCAN colloquium series.
