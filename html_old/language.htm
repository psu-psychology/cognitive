Language

 

One of the major research tracks in the Cognitive program focuses on the study of language. Our group studies a broad range of topics in psycholinguistics. These topics include comparative studies of speech perception, first and second language acquisition, speech production, and bilingualism. Our research populations are equally diverse and include human infants, adults (from Penn State University and around the globe), as well as nonhuman primates. Students entering our program gain exposure to a range of approaches including behavioral, eye-tracking, looking-time, and playback methods. There are frequent opportunities for graduate students to interact with visiting scholars here at Penn State and on research visits to collaborating laboratories in the US and Europe. Members of area meet weekly to discuss current research and papers through participation in the Language Science Research group.

The Language Science Research group is a large interdisciplinary community of language researchers from many departments across the university. The goal of this group is to foster research activities and allow group members to interact and collaborate with others who may share similar interests. To that effect, the group meets weekly to discuss current research and to host visiting speakers. For more information on the Language Science research group, please visit their website (coming soon).
